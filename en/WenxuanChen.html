Profile

As one of the youngest Standing Fellow of China Intellectual Property Society, Dr. Wenxuan Chen has triple work experience as prestigious judge, senior legal counsel and lawyer. He has extensive practical experience across different industries, businesses, laws and regions.


Dr. Chen once served as a presiding judge at the Intellectual Property Division of The First Intermediate People’s Court of Beijing, where he adjudicated more than 800 cases regarding IP, antitrust, and commercial affairs. He also participated in the drafting of a number of judicial policies and interpretations under the organization of Supreme People’s Court of China, including the China's Intellectual Property Judicial Policy, the Judiciary Interpretation of Patent Infringements, Patent Authorization and Prosecution, Trademark Authorization and Prosecution, E-Commerce, and Right to Network Dissemination of Information, etc.


Serving as a senior legal counsel at Alibaba Group from 2014, Dr. Chen took the position of the chief consel of Intellectual Property Protection Team as well as Nonstandard Dispute Resolution Team, being responsible for litigation and non-litigation affairs concerning E-commerce, cloud computing, logistics, digital entertainment, film & televisions publishing, health & medical, big data industry, social media, marketing, E-finance and other multiple industries.


In addition to Standing Fellow of China Intellectual Property Society, Dr. Chen is a practical tutor at Law School of Renmin University of China, an arbitrator of South China International Economic and Trade Arbitration Commission, as well as an external expert of Patent Examination Cooperation Center, Intellectual Property Office of China.

Dr. Chen published his magnum opus Patent Boundary - Interpretation of Claims and Policy Adjustment of Protection Scope ( Intellectual Property Publishing House, 2014), and co-authored 5 books including Selected Chinese Patent Cases which has an English version published by Wolters Kluwer (2014). He once participated in 17 research projects with national, provincial and municipal level, and published more than 30 dissertations. Dr. Chen is also the co-inventor of 8 patents/patent applications.
